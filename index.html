Latent-GRM: Latent Gaussian Reconstruction Model for High-Quality 3D Reconstruction

Anonymous



Latent-GRM enables fast and high-quality 3D Gaussian reconstruction from sparse-view images, rendering high-fidelity normal maps, and extracting meshes, and can work in synergy with other generation models for text-to-3D and image-to-3D generation.



Abstract


We propose Latent-GRM, a Latent Gaussian Reconstruction Model that can reconstruct high-quality 3D assets from sparse-view images within a second. Existing methods for generalizable 3D reconstruction suffer from slow inference and rendering speeds due to inefficient volumetric rendering and mainly focus on modeling in RGB space, without view-dependent modeling. In this work, we propose 3D latent Gaussians that model view-dependent information and introduce uncertainty to model the semantic distribution around the position of Gaussian. Then, we construct a transformer-based reconstruction model to map RGB images to view-dependent latent Gaussians, which can be rendered and decoded by a lightweight convolution-based architecture. Extensive results demonstrate that our method achieves comparable results to the current state-of-the-art reconstruction model in sparse-view 3D reconstruction. We also showcase the potential of Latent-GRM in generative tasks such as text-to-3D and image-to-3D, by combining it with existing multi-view diffusion models.


Method


Our method predicts the 3D latent Gaussian parameters from sparse posed images. We use a VAE to compress high-resolution images into low-resolution features. Then, we use a transformer-based model to reconstruct the latent space. We back-project the processed pixels into the latent Gaussians and render feature maps, normal maps, and alpha maps.
